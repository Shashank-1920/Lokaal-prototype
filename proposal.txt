The problem is not a lack of information on the internet; it is a lack of curated, student-specific, locally accurate information in one accessible place.Three specific pain points we have experienced ourselves:Discovery friction — finding even a basic mess or grocery shop near campus requires asking seniors or trial-and-error. Google Maps shows options but not which ones are actually good, affordable, or student-friendly.The auto tax — the single biggest practical problem for students in Enathur is transport cost. A trip to Kanchipuram for groceries or stationery costs Rs. 300-400 in auto charges alone, which on a student budget is a serious recurring expense.No student-centric travel resource — students want affordable weekend trips from campus but have no reliable source for routes, costs, timings, and student-budget breakdowns.Core Insight:  The student is the target user. We are not outsiders trying to understand this problem — we live it every day. That is our unfair advantage.02  |  The ProposalLokaal — A student city platform built around four pillarsLokaal is a web-first platform that serves as the definitive local guide and utility layer for students in any Indian college town. The platform launches in Kanchipuram as a pilot, with a clear template to expand to any college city in Tamil Nadu and beyond.PillarSectionWhat It SolvesRevenue Model01Food & RestaurantsWhere to eat, price range, student-friendly options near campusFeatured listings, paid badges02Shopping GuideWhere to buy stationery, clothes, electronics, essentials in townFeatured listings, deals section03Places & Travel RoutesWhat to visit in and around Kanchipuram, weekend trip guides with student budgetsAffiliate travel bookings, sponsored routes04Hyperlocal DeliveryOrder groceries or essentials from Enathur shops, delivered by campus riders for Rs. 40-50 vs Rs. 300-400 auto farePer-delivery commission (Rs. 15-20 per order)The delivery section is the anchor of the business model in Phase 1. Have explicit WhatsApp agreement on the split.Test the manual delivery flow on WhatsApp — take 5 test orders, coordinate manually, measure what breaks.Phase 1  —  MVP Build  (Weeks 3-6)Goal:  Ship a working platform with real listings and a functional delivery ordering flow. Imperfect but live.Shashank builds the frontend — home page with 4 categories, listing cards with photo, description, price, maps link and tags, and mobile-first responsive layout.Karthikeya builds the backend — Firebase schema, admin panel to add/edit listings from the Google Sheet, deployment on Firebase Hosting.Delivery section MVP: simple order form (item, quantity, shop name, room number), WhatsApp notification to the assigned rider, UPI payment upfront. No complex real-time tracking needed at this stage.Ajay and Sandeep continue adding content — target 60 listings at launch across all four sections. Write one insider tip per listing.End-of-phase review: all four team members test every flow, log bugs, fix before launch.Phase 2  —  Launch & Early Revenue  (Weeks 7-10)Goal:  Get live, get users, get the first paying business. Use delivery revenue as proof for listing partnerships.Launch via college WhatsApp groups, notice board QR codes, and word of mouth. Target: 300+ unique visitors in the first two weeks.Run delivery service actively — even 5 orders per day at Rs. 50 each generates Rs. 7,500 per month in gross delivery revenue.After 30-45 days, walk into small Kanchipuram shops with: delivery transaction screenshots, platform visitor count from Google Analytics, and a clear Rs. 500/month featured listing pitch.Target: 3 paying businesses by end of Month 2. This is your proof of income — screenshot every UPI transfer and maintain a simple revenue log.Set up Google Analytics for traffic data and add a Suggest a Place form to begin community contribution.Phase 3  —  Scale & Deepen  (Months 3-5)Goal:  Expand to more Kanchipuram businesses, introduce student deals, and prepare the template for the next city.Introduce a Deals section — partner shops offer student discounts (show app = 10% off). Students return daily to access deals, not just for discovery.Add user reviews and community tips with moderation by Ajay and Sandeep.Expand the travel routes section with documented weekend trips — Mahabalipuram, Pondicherry, Tiruvannamalai, Chennai — with student budget breakdowns.Begin conversations with Vellore and Tiruchirappalli student networks. Identify a content team in each city using the same model (local students as researchers).Shashank and Karthikeya build the PWA / mobile-optimised app layer. Push notifications for new deals and delivery status updates.Phase 4  —  Full Commercialisation  (Month 6+)Goal:  Three revenue streams running, multi-city presence, and university partnership conversations in progress.Revenue stream 1: Paid featured listings across all cities — Rs. 500 to Rs. 2,000 per month per business depending on category and footfall.Revenue stream 2: Affiliate commissions — Swiggy and Zomato both have affiliate programs. Every order placed through a platform referral link earns a commission per transaction.Revenue stream 3: University tie-ups — pitch to university welcome and hostel offices to include Lokaal in fresher orientation packs. One institutional deal can be worth Rs. 10,000 to Rs. 50,000 per year.Begin preparing an investor-ready deck with actual revenue data, user growth curve, and the city expansion roadmap.04  |  Clear-Cut RoadmapTimelineMilestones & DeliverablesWeek 1-2  (Phase 0)Domain registered.
